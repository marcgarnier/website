Research paper · COM 885 · Université de Sherbrooke

“That’s kino anon” — reading charts as gamified devices on 4chan’s /lit/.

A communication-research study of the “charts” circulating on 4chan’s Literature board. How do these playful visual reading lists frame and diffuse literary radicalism inside an anonymous online counter-public?

Research question

When a reading list becomes political communication.

On /lit/, users share charts — small PNG or JPEG images that rank authors and books along logics of progression and hierarchy. The paper argues that these visuals do more than recommend references: they structure paths of initiation and distinction within an anonymous public, turning literature into an indirect political language.

Central claim — “How is radicalism circulated through the form of the chart?”

Reading is reframed as a quest: you climb from tier to tierProgression, unlock authors and become “proficient”Reward, follow arrows, levels and colour codesSimplification, and enter the closed circle of “real readers”Community.

Theoretical framework

Reading /lit/ as a digital counter-public.

The analysis mobilises theories of the public sphere and its fragmentation to make sense of an anonymous, affective and highly symbolic discursive arena.

01

Fragmented public spheres

Bennett & Pfetsch (2018): political communication now unfolds across dispersed micro-publics rather than one common arena.

02

Counter-publics

Fraser (2001): subordinate groups build parallel discursive arenas to circulate their own counter-discourses — /lit/ as a contemporary illustration.

03

Pseudo-public sphere

De Zeeuw (2024): spaces that imitate the mechanisms of public debate without honouring its deliberative rules — where nothing is quite serious, nothing quite play.

04

Gamification & ludification

Deterding et al. (2011) and Raessens (2014): game-design logics applied to non-game contexts, reconfiguring culture as a playful, identity-driven pursuit.

05

Framing

Entman (2004); Gestlé & Piar (2020): the chart as a framing device — visual hierarchy replacing rational deliberation, an “illusion of choice”.

06

Trolling & transgression

Phillips (2015) and Nagle (2017): 4chan’s subcultural trolling and “culture of transgression” as the backdrop against which irony masks radical discourse.

Analysis · gamification

Four recurring gamified mechanics.

Charts are coded against the game-design elements described by Deterding, Dixon, Khaled & Nacke (2011) — now through the computational pipeline described below. Four mechanics recur across the corpus.

Mechanic · 01

Progression

From novice to expert: the reader advances level by level along a literary path, choosing to stop, continue or branch off.

Mechanic · 02

Symbolic reward

Mastery of a corpus is staged as an achievement — becoming “proficient”, earned through the “grind” of reading.

Mechanic · 03

Cognitive simplification

Ideological complexity is turned into clear visual schemas: arrows, tiers, categories and colour codes.

Mechanic · 04

Community identification

A ready-made “panoply” of authors that rewards the status of the initiate — the “real reader” inside a closed circle.

Analysis · framing structures

Three types of chart.

Beyond their shared mechanics, the charts fall into three framing structures — from guided learning to open-looking exploration.

Type 01 · learning & hierarchy

Initiatory guides

Teaching codes — arrows, chronological order, practical advice. A graded, gamified learning experience where each reading unlocks the next and builds a community of self-taught experts.

Type 02 · prescription

Cultural canons

A closed ideological library (traditionalist, libertarian, communist…) whose works reinforce one another. The chart becomes a framing device (Entman): visual hierarchy replaces rational deliberation.

Type 03 · exploration & simulation

Playful cartographies

Decision trees and RPG-style skill trees where the user “chooses” a route. The apparent freedom of the player masks an orientation framed in advance by the chart’s author.

Corpus & method · revised

A computational pipeline for the charts of /lit/.

This revised method keeps the theoretical objects of the original project — Deterding et al.’s (2011) gamified mechanics, Entman’s (2004) framing structures and the counter-public reading (Fraser, 2001; Bennett & Pfetsch, 2018) — but replaces manual coding with a reproducible, zero-budget computational chain, built exclusively on open-source tools and open-weight models from GitHub and Hugging Face. Everything runs in Python on a personal machine; a consumer GPU suffices, and Colab or Kaggle provide free ones.

01

Automated corpus construction

The 262-chart base is kept but rebuilt and extended systematically, via 4chan’s public JSON API and the third-party archives indexing /lit/ over the long run (Warosu, Desuarchive, queried through FoolFuuka). Keyword search retrieves not only images but their circulation metadata: source thread, date, replies, reception vocabulary (“that’s kino anon”). Perceptual hashing then turns deduplication into a measure of diffusion — repost frequency of a hash becomes a quantitative indicator of a chart’s circulation, and Hamming distance between hashes reconstructs chart genealogies.

4chan API · BASC-py4chan · FoolFuuka · imagehash
02

Structured extraction — OCR + vision-language models

Human reading of each image is replaced by two automated layers. First OCR (PaddleOCR, Surya) — more robust than Tesseract on stylised fonts — extracts all text with per-block coordinates. Then a locally run open-weight VLM (Qwen2.5-VL, InternVL2, LLaVA-NeXT) emits structured JSON per chart: layout type, nodes with positions, relations between nodes (arrows, reading order, branches), ordinal labels and colour codes. The result is a formal representation of each chart — a directed graph whose nodes are works and whose edges are prescribed reading paths. Entities are normalised by fuzzy matching against Open Library / Wikidata dumps.

PaddleOCR · Surya · Qwen2.5-VL · rapidfuzz
03

Automated coding of mechanics, human-validated

The Deterding codebook becomes an operationalisable annotation scheme, each mechanic receiving computable indicators (detailed below). LLM coding is not taken at face value: following Ziems et al. (2024), a subsample of ~50 charts is double-coded by hand and human–machine agreement is measured with Cohen’s κ or Krippendorff’s α. The prompt is iterated until α ≥ 0.70, then applied to the whole corpus — the original manual method thus becomes the validation procedure for the computational one.

Ziems et al., 2024 · κ / α ≥ 0.70
04

Inductive typology, tested against theory

Rather than imposing the three framing types, the pipeline puts them to the test. Each chart is a vector combining structural features from its graph (depth, width, tree vs grid, cycles), a visual embedding (CLIP, SigLIP, DINOv2) and a textual embedding of the OCR output. Unsupervised clustering (HDBSCAN) and BERTopic surface inductive groupings. The question becomes empirical: do the emergent clusters match the three postulated structures? Correspondence is measured by Adjusted Rand Index, turning the typology into a testable hypothesis rather than an a priori grid.

HDBSCAN · BERTopic · CLIP / DINOv2 · ARI
05

Network analysis — mapping ideological libraries

Structured extraction enables what manual coding could not: an author co-occurrence network across the entire corpus, two authors linked when they share a chart. Community detection (Louvain, Leiden) makes the “closed ideological libraries” appear empirically as dense modules — traditionalist, libertarian, communist. Betweenness centrality identifies the bridge authors connecting “neutral” literature to radical corpora: precisely where the circulation of radicalism plays out, now visible and quantifiable. Combined with the temporal data of step 01, it tracks how canons evolve.

networkx · Gephi · Louvain / Leiden
06

Interpretation & reproducibility

The counter-public interpretation is unchanged, but now rests on quantified results: prevalence of each mechanic, cluster structure, network topology, repost dynamics. The central argument — the message lies as much in the form as in the content — gains empirical grounding, since form is formally encoded and its association with ideological content becomes statistically testable. The whole pipeline is published on GitHub: collection scripts, prompts, operationalised codebook, notebooks, data versioned with DVC. No commercial API is required, which guarantees reproducibility, result stability, and ethical compliance — the data never leaves the researcher’s machine, no small matter for a 4chan corpus.

Operationalising the four mechanics.

Indicator · 01

Progression

Ordinal labels detected by OCR (tier, level, novice/proficient, start/end) and the existence of a directed path in the extracted graph — is it a DAG with a source and sinks?

Indicator · 02

Symbolic reward

Lexical detection of achievement vocabulary (proficient, mastery, patrician) by a zero- or few-shot classifier built on an open text LLM, applied to the OCR output.

Indicator · 03

Cognitive simplification

Visual indicators computed with OpenCV: dominant colour count, density of arrows and separators, image-to-text ratio. Simplification becomes a measurable variable, not an impression.

Indicator · 04

Community identification

Detection of in-group lexicon and /lit/ subcultural markers, both inside the chart and in the threads that receive it — using the circulation metadata from step 01.

Limits and safeguards.

Limit 01

OCR on ornamented fonts

Charts favour stylised typography — the Nietzsche guide is a case in point. Hence the dual OCR + VLM layer and sample-based human validation.

Limit 02

VLM hallucination

Models can invent plausible but absent titles. Normalisation against Open Library acts as a filter, and every unmatched title is checked by hand.

Limit 03

Inherited model bias

LLM coding inherits the biases of its models. Human–machine agreement scores and full publication of the prompts are the minimum defensible safeguards.

Conclusion

Findings and an opening.

Findings

Radicalism as an economy of symbols

The visual, gamified form of the charts makes literary radicalism seductive, shareable and culturally valued. Diffusion owes less to explicit militancy than to an economy of symbols and provocation that rewards subcultural belonging — radicalism that “exposes without imposing”.

Opening

Next research

Almost no scholarship targets /lit/ directly. A promising next step is to analyse 4chan’s own structure — its code, UI/UX and affordances — to better understand the phenomena that emerge from it.
